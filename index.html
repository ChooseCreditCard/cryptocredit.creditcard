How Crypto-Linked Credit Cards Actually Work

Crypto credit cards let you spend crypto through familiar Visa/Mastercard rails. Here’s how conversion, spreads, stablecoins, rewards and compliance really function behind the scenes.

Explore Crypto & Web3 Cards

What Is a Crypto Credit Card?

These cards are traditional payment cards linked to a wallet or exchange, converting crypto to fiat at checkout. This makes them compatible with global card networks while allowing users to spend digital assets.

Most do not settle on-chain. Instead, the issuer performs internal conversion and uses standard banking rails for merchant settlement.

How Crypto Conversions Happen

When you tap the card, the issuer checks your crypto balance, converts an equivalent amount to fiat, and approves the transaction. The conversion can be:

  • Real-time conversion (JIT)
  • Pre-loaded conversions done earlier
  • Stablecoin conversion for reduced volatility

Spreads and fees vary by provider and can significantly affect real value versus advertised rewards.

Risks, Tax & Compliance

Crypto spending may trigger taxable disposals depending on jurisdiction. Some cards generate dozens of micro-events per month. Always check your local rules.

Volatility also matters: asset value may rise or fall between loading and spending. Spreads, FX fees, and liquidity constraints further impact real cost.
